How to Choose Your Protocol: A Beginner's Guide

February 2026 · 5 min read · Tundrex Editorial Team

One of the first questions new Tundrex customers ask is straightforward: where do I start? It's a reasonable question. Three products. Multiple protocols. Different combinations for different health goals. For someone encountering this for the first time, the system can feel complex.

It doesn't have to be. This guide walks you through the logic of the Tundrex protocol system from the ground up — so you can make a confident, informed choice based on where you are and what you're trying to achieve.

Why Three Products?

The digestive tract is not one uniform environment. The small intestine and the large intestine (colon) are fundamentally different — different pH levels, different microbial populations, different immune functions, and different physiological roles.

Most probiotic products target only one zone, or don't specify at all. The Tundrex approach is different: each product is formulated with a specific bacterial strain that is most active in a specific section of the GI tract. This allows protocols to be built sequentially, covering the full digestive system in a targeted, phase-by-phase approach.

The Three Products Explained

Tundrex 1.1 delivers Bacillus subtilis — our foundational, flagship strain — which is most active in the small intestine. This is the starting point for every Tundrex protocol. The small intestine is where the majority of nutrient absorption happens and where much of the gut-associated immune tissue (GALT) is concentrated. Starting here establishes the microbial foundation for everything that follows.

Tundrex 3 delivers Bacillus amyloliquefaciens, most active in the large intestine (colon). The colon is home to the densest microbial community in the body — roughly 99% of the gut microbiome lives here. Disruptions to this community, including from antibiotic use, illness, and poor diet, can have far-reaching consequences. Tundrex 3 targets this zone specifically, working as Phase 2 in our more intensive protocols.

Tundrex 4 delivers Bacillus amyloliquefaciens in a formulation that is most active in the small intestine. It serves as the final phase in our Return to Well-Being and GIT Support protocols, completing a full-system restoration cycle by returning to the small intestine with a different species than in Phase 1.

The Sequential Logic

Think of the protocol system as moving through the digestive tract in sequence: Tundrex 1.1 (small intestine) → Tundrex 3 (large intestine) → Tundrex 4 (small intestine again, different strain). Used together, the three products provide complete digestive tract coverage with complementary bacterial species.

The Three Protocols

Protocols are pre-structured combinations of products, each designed for a specific health goal and duration:

Immunity Boost — 1 box of Tundrex 1.1, 10 days. This is the recommended entry point for most people. It provides the foundational small intestine support that primes your GALT and establishes a microbial baseline. Ideal for general immune maintenance, seasonal use, or as a first-time experience with Tundrex. Recommended to repeat every 3–4 months.

Microbiome Recovery — Tundrex 1.1 (×2) + Tundrex 3 (×1), 20 days. A more intensive protocol for people whose gut health has been significantly disrupted — by antibiotics, illness, surgery, or prolonged dietary imbalance. The double dose of 1.1 builds a strong foundation in the small intestine before Tundrex 3 addresses the large intestine. Recommended every 20–24 weeks.

Return to Well-Being — Tundrex 1.1 (×3) + Tundrex 3 (×1) + Tundrex 4 (×1), 35 days. The most comprehensive Tundrex protocol, covering the full digestive tract in a structured three-phase sequence. Designed for people recovering from significant illness (including post-viral conditions), those with complex gut dysfunction, or those committed to the most thorough microbiome restoration available. The 35-day structure gives each phase time to establish before moving to the next.

Which Protocol Is Right for You?

  • New to Tundrex / General wellness: Start with Immunity Boost
  • Recent antibiotic course / Post-illness: Microbiome Recovery
  • Chronic gut issues / Post-viral fatigue / Full reset: Return to Well-Being
  • Unsure? Start with Immunity Boost and see how you respond before moving to a longer protocol

How to Take Tundrex

Each product comes with 50 capsules and clear protocol instructions. In general: take on an empty stomach, 30 minutes before eating, once per day. The spore format means the capsules are shelf-stable — no refrigeration required.

Because spore-based probiotics work differently from conventional probiotics, the timeline for noticing results is also different. Some people notice changes in digestive comfort and energy within the first week. For more significant gut restoration — particularly after antibiotic use or post-viral illness — allow the full protocol course before evaluating.

Can You Take Individual Products Without a Protocol?

Yes. Each product can be purchased individually and used as a standalone supplement. If, for example, you want to focus specifically on large intestine support, Tundrex 3 can be used on its own. However, the protocol sequences are designed to maximize the cumulative effect, and most customers see the best results when following the complete multi-phase structure.

When in Doubt, Start Simple

The Immunity Boost protocol exists precisely for moments of uncertainty. It's a low-commitment 10-day course that introduces your gut to Bacillus subtilis without a significant investment of time or money. It's the most common starting point among new Tundrex customers — and for most people, it's a natural first step toward whatever protocol comes next.
