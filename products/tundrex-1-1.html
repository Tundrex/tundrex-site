About Tundrex 1.1

Tundrex 1.1 is the core product in the Tundrex family — a spore-forming probiotic supplement featuring Bacillus subtilis, a highly resilient strain of bacteria sourced from untouched organic tundra soil in Siberia. Frequently Asked Questions

What is Tundrex 1.1? +
Tundrex 1.1 is a probiotic food supplement containing Bacillus subtilis — a spore-forming strain of beneficial bacteria sourced from pristine organic tundra soil in Siberia. It is active in the small intestine and helps support gut microbiome balance and immune function.
How is Tundrex 1.1 different from other probiotics? +
Tundrex 1.1 features Bacillus subtilis — a spore-forming strain that is significantly more resilient than common Lactobacillus strains found in most probiotics. It survives stomach acid, requires no refrigeration, and is sourced exclusively from pure Siberian tundra soil rather than lab-cultivated or dairy-based strains.
Can I take Tundrex 1.1 with antibiotics? +
It is best to avoid taking Tundrex 1.1 simultaneously with antibiotics. While there is no health risk, antibiotics will kill the probiotic bacteria, reducing effectiveness. We recommend completing your antibiotic course before beginning a Tundrex regimen — or starting Tundrex once antibiotics are finished. The Microbiome Recovery Protocol is specifically designed for post-antibiotic recovery.
Does Tundrex 1.1 need to be refrigerated? +
No. Tundrex 1.1 is a spore-forming probiotic, which means it remains stable and viable at room temperature for up to 2 years. No refrigeration is required.
Are there any side effects? +
As a natural probiotic supplement, Tundrex 1.1 has no known side effects and no reported cases of overdose. It is a food supplement, not a medication. If you have specific health concerns, consult with your healthcare provider before use. Note that the product contains small amounts of corn, potato, and sugar.
How often should I take Tundrex 1.1? +
For general immune and gut support, one course (50 capsules over 10 days) every 3–4 months is recommended. This is the Immunity Boost protocol. For deeper microbiome restoration, consider the Microbiome Recovery or Return to Well-Being protocols.
What is the difference between Tundrex 1.1, 3, and 4? +
Tundrex 1.1 delivers Bacillus subtilis and is active in the small intestine. Tundrex 3 delivers Bacillus amyloliquefaciens and is active in the large intestine. Tundrex 4 delivers Bacillus amyloliquefaciens and is active in the small intestine. Together, they complement each other across the full digestive tract and are used in combination within the advanced protocols.
